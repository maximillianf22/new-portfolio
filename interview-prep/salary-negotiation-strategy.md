## Salary Negotiation Strategy

### 1. Research Market Rates

**For Senior Frontend Developer in LATAM (Remote US companies):**
- Entry Senior: $2,500-3,500/month
- Mid Senior: $3,500-4,500/month
- Senior/Lead: $4,500-6,000+/month

**Your target:** $3,000-4,000/month minimum (based on your experience)

### 2. Value Proposition

**What you bring:**
- 10+ years experience
- Leadership experience (led teams of 5-16 people)
- Multiple tech stacks (React, Next.js, Vue.js, TypeScript)
- AI integration experience (rare skill)
- Multiple industries (trading, fintech, e-commerce, AI)
- Storybook/documentation expertise
- Clean code practices (ESLint, Prettier)
- Migration experience (Vue 2‚Üí3, PHP‚ÜíReact, .NET‚ÜíReact)

**This is worth $3,500-4,500/month minimum!**

### 3. Salary Negotiation Script

**When asked about salary expectations:**

```
"I'm currently earning around $4,000/month across my consulting projects, 
but I'm looking to consolidate into a single focused role. 

Based on my experience:
- 10+ years in frontend development
- Leadership experience (teams of 5-16 people)
- Multiple tech stacks (React, Next.js, Vue.js)
- AI integration expertise
- Architecture and migration experience

I'm looking for a role in the $3,500-4,500/month range, 
depending on the scope and responsibilities."
```

**Key points:**
- Mention current total (shows you're not desperate)
- Frame as "consolidating" not "needing more"
- Give range, not exact number
- Tie salary to value you bring

### 4. Red Flags to Avoid

**Don't accept:**
- Less than $3,000/month (you're worth more)
- "We'll review in 6 months" (get it in writing or don't accept)
- Equity instead of salary (unless it's a startup you believe in)

**Do negotiate:**
- Base salary first
- Then benefits (health, PTO, etc.)
- Then growth opportunities

## Red Flags in Job Postings

**Avoid:**
- "Competitive salary" without range
- "Startup equity" as main compensation
- "We're like a family" (often means poor boundaries)
- Unclear job descriptions
- No mention of remote work flexibility

**Look for:**
- Clear salary range
- Remote-first or remote-friendly
- Modern tech stack mentioned
- Growth opportunities
- Benefits clearly stated
